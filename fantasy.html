Our Favourites

THE HOBBIT

It is a classic fantasy novel, published in 1937, narrating the story of Bilbo Baggins, a hobbit who sets off on an adventure quite unexpectedly with a group of dwarves to reclaim their homeland from the dragon Smaug. On his journey, Bilbo runs through various dangers, finds a magical ring, and proves his courage. A cornerstone of the fantasy genre, the story concludes with Bilbo returning home, changed and wiser.

HARRY POTTER AND THE SORCERERS STONE

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone is a fantasy and adventure novel that introduces Harry Potter, an orphaned boy who learns on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard. Attending Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, Harry discovers a plot regarding a powerful magical object, the Sorcerer's Stone, and thwarts the dark wizard Voldemort from regaining power with the help of his friends Ron and Hermione. The novel combines elements of fantasy, mystery, and coming-of-age themes, and it has been in print since 1997.

A GAME OF THRONES

A Game of Thrones is the first in George R.R. Martin's A Song of Ice and Fire epic fantasy series and was published in 1996. This is a book about the continents of Westeros and Essos, with many houses battling for the right to rule from the Iron Throne. It's a novel that combines medieval fantasy, political intrigue, and complex character dynamics-which is well known for its dark themes and unpredictable plot twists.
