CLASSIC

The classic book generally denotes texts that are seldom rewritten and include a universal theme, deep characterization, and timelessness in thoughts on human beings. These texts, normally written several hundred years ago, express the cultural and social norms of their era yet can be reinterpreted down the ages.

FANTASY

Fantasies are set in imaginary magical kingdoms, demons, and the supernatural, sometimes in another world or era. They offer epic drama, magic spells, and the struggle between good and evil. Many such novels are about the struggle for power, fate, and heroism, offering an escape to worlds of wonder and adventure.

ROMANCE

Romance is the relation between two individuals, and it deals with love, lust, and personal growth. The genre revolves around the emotional development of their bond that usually culminates in a happy ending including intimacy, joy, and the promise of a lasting, committed relationship full of elation and fulfillment.
